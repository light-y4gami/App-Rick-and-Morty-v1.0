Rick and Morty

Rick and Morty is an American adult animated television series created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon in 2013 for Adult Swim, it also aired on Cartoon Network. The series follows the misadventures of a scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his easily influenced grandson, Morty, who balance their time between domestic life and space, time and intergalactic travel. Dan Harmon, the co-creator of the series and Justin Roiland are responsible for the main voices of Morty and Rick, the series also includes the voices of Chris Parnell, Spencer Grammer and Sarah Chalke. The series originated from a scrappy short animated by Roiland for the Channel 101 film festival called Doc and Mharti, a parody of the main leads from Back to the Future. When Harmon was fired by NBC in 2013 over arguments between him and the executives of the series he had created, Community, he and Roiland developed a series based on characters already created by Roiland. The sixth season premiered on September 4, 2022, and consists of ten episodes. A seventh season was confirmed as part of a long-term deal with Cartoon Network that ordered 70 new episodes, which renewed the series for a tenth season. Adult Swim cut ties with Justin Roiland in 2023 amid allegations of gender-based violence and announced that he would reprise his roles.

Story lines

Rick Sanchez is an example of the typical "mad scientist." He's a genius, but he's irresponsible, alcoholic, selfish, a bit depressed, and lacking in sanity. Rick, for different reasons, ends up moving to his daughter's house, Beth's, and at that moment he meets his grandson, Morty's; a 14-year-old boy, shy and not very bright. Teaming up with his grandson, Rick and Morty go on a variety of adventures throughout the cosmos and parallel universes. And it is through so many experiences and reflections that Rick wants his grandson Morty not to end up like his father, Jerry, a very unsuccessful man who, despite having good intentions, turns out to be quite useless on many occasions and depends a lot on his wife, Beth, Rick's daughter.

Despite being very close, Rick and his grandson experience moments where Morty's sister Summer sometimes joins in on Rick's quaint adventures.

Writing

The general formula in Rick and Morty consists of the juxtaposition of two scenarios that come together. On the one hand, it shows the adventures that an extremely selfish and alcoholic grandfather lives through the vast and unknown intergalactic or interdimensional space, which drags his grandson to travel with him. These adventures are interspersed in each episode with family domestic dramas more typical of the sitcom genre. Dan Harmon describes this fusion of science fiction and family atmosphere as a mixture of influences between the two works of Matt Groening: The Simpsons and Futurama. On the other hand, co-author Justin Roiland communicated the intention of the creators that the series surpass the traditional continuity on television, thus opting for discontinuous storylines, "not fixed by rules". Roiland describes each episode as "his own point of view of him" of him.
